Pat McAfee | Net Worth, NFL Contracts & Other Earnings

Net Worth

An American former football player, Pat McAfee’s net worth as of December 2023 is a whopping $20 million.

The guy has amassed a massive fortune from his multifaceted career as a sports analyst, wrestling commentator, and podcaster.

Similarly, he also owns and runs a business which contributes to a large chunk of his income.

NFL Contract

On April 25, 2009, Pat was drafted by Indiana Colts in the round 7 and 222 overall pickups.

He signed a four-year $1.808 million rookie contract with Colts on July 29, 2009.

The contract included $58,280 signing bonus, $452,000 average annual salary and $58,280 guarantee.

After the rookie contract expired in 2012, Pat signed an extension with Colts on March 1, 2013.

The one-year franchise tag contract was worth $2.977 million with the same amount of annual salary and guarantee.

Nonetheless, Pat signed the most lucrative contract of his career in 2014 with the Colts.

Colts signed Pat on a five-year $14.5 million contract on March 7, 2014.

The contract came with all the necessary bells and whistles, including a $1 million signing bonus, $2.9 million average annual salaries, $3.75 million guaranteed at signing, and a $3.75 million total guarantee.

So if we take a look at Pat’s earning timeline with Colts, he made $13,481,764 on salary, $1,058,280 on signing bonuses, and $500,000 on incentives over the course of eight seasons.

Similarly, his total earnings from the deal were $15,040,044, which is not the highest amount for an NFL player but still a respectable figure.

Pat McAfee | Lifestyle

Pat McAfee made many plays for the Colts and fulfilled all the dreams he had as a teenager.

McAfee currently spends most of his time helping those in need and his The Pat McAfee Show.

Likewise, he’s had his share of ups and downs in life.

In February 2019, Pat proposed to his girlfriend, Samantha Ludy. The sweet couple got married in August 2020 at Coxhall Gardens in Carmel, Indiana, United States.

Moreover, McAfee is not just another football player.

Interestingly, Pat became the Guinness World Record holder by successfully kicking a 40-yard field goal blindfolded on October 20, 2010.

Nonetheless, his 40-yard field goal record was beaten by Davis Brief on September 23, 2018, as he kicked a 45-yard field goal while blindfolded.

Furthermore, Pat was arrested and charged with public intoxication, a Class B misdemeanor, on October 20, 2010.

He apparently swam in a canal in Board Ripple and was wet and shirtless when found by a woman at roughly 5:15 in the morning.

When police tested for intoxication, 0.15 blood alcohol content was found in his system. Consequently, he received a one-game suspension with Colts for his action and was fined approximately $23,235.

McAfee issued an apology notice after two weeks of being arrested, stating what he had done was wrong and was a dumb decision.

He also stated that he would never do anything that would embarrass his team.

Pat McAfee | Podcasts, Wrestling, and FanDuel Deal

Podcasts

Many of us have often found ourselves in an unfamiliar situation where we have no idea how to proceed further.

Punter for the Colts was in a similar situation after he decided to hang his boots and retire early from football.

When he decided to retire, he actually had to Google to find out the procedure of retiring early.

And shortly after retiring from the National Football League, McAfee joined Barstool Sports and launched The Pat McAfee Show for Barstool’s channel on Sirius.

However, in 2018, he announced that his relationship with the management of Barstool Sports was severed over his pay disagreement.

After quitting Barstool Sports, McAfee launched PMI or Pat McAfee Inc.

He also launched several self-produced podcasts, most notably “The Pod,” which focused on gambling, and “The Pat McAfee Show 2.0”.

Likewise, he began hosting a show on Sirius again in 2020.

Wrestling

Over his numerous stints post-NFL career, wrestling is also one of them.

He has worked as a pro wrestling analyst.

He was appointed as a color commentator alongside Michael Cole.

Similarly, on March 3, 2022, the CEO of WWE, Mr. Vince McMahon, appeared on The Pat McAfee Show.

Vince offered Pat a match at the upcoming WrestleMania 38 during the interview.
