What was Ray Lewis III cause of death?

Ray Lewis III, the oldest son of renowned Baltimore Ravens linebacker and Hall of Fame inductee Ray Lewis, has sadly passed away.

The devastating news was confirmed by his younger brother, Rahsaan Lewis, through social media.

Ray III was 28 years old at the time of his death.

Ray Lewis III cause of death

Cause of Death Unspecified

At the time of writing, the specific cause of Ray Lewis III’s death has not been publicly disclosed.

He had recently celebrated his 28th birthday on June 5, just days before the heartbreaking news emerged.

Touching Tributes and Heartfelt Messages

Following Ray III’s passing, many individuals, including his brother Rahsaan, shared touching tributes in his memory.

Rahsaan expressed his disbelief and conveyed his love for his late brother, emphasizing the pain he was enduring.

Their sister, Diaymon Lewis, also paid tribute to Ray III, professing her love and stating that he will forever hold a special place in her heart.

Ray III showcased his athletic talent as a standout running back at Lake Mary Prep in Florida.

He continued his football career by joining the Miami Hurricanes football team at the University of Miami, following in his father’s footsteps.

However, his collegiate journey took a turn when he transferred to Coastal Carolina University in 2015.

Unfortunately, his time there was cut short due to his dismissal from the football team and the university.

This was after an indictment by a South Carolina grand jury on a charge of third-degree criminal sexual assault.

Eventually, the charges were dropped after an extensive investigation.

Ray III concluded his college football career with the Virginia Union Panthers in 2017.

He then later played professional indoor football for the Wyoming Mustangs in 2021.
