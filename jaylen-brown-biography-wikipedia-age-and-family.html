Jaylen Brown Biography | Wikipedia Age And Family

The Boston Celtics of the National Basketball Association (NBA) are the team for American professional basketball player Jaylen Marselles Brown, also known by his stage as Jaylen Brown.

He played collegiate basketball at UC Berkeley for one season after being recognized as a five-star recruit by Rivals.com in the class of 2015 seniors. When he was 22 years old, Jaylen was elected as the National Basketball Players Association’s youngest vice president.

Workplace Career:

On June 23rd, the Boston Celtics made Jaylen Brown the third overall pick in the 2016 NBA Draft. After averaging 16.0 points, 6.2 rebounds, and 2.3 steals in six Summer League games, Brown signed his rookie scale contract with the Celtics. On October 26 against the Brooklyn Nets, he played in his first game for the Celtics, scoring nine points on 3-for-4 shooting and adding two blocks in more than 19 minutes.

On November 3rd, Brown made his first career start against the Cleveland Cavaliers, scoring 19 points. He played in 78 games for the Celtics during the regular season, making 20 starts. His stats on the court were 17.2 minutes, 6.6 points, 2.8 rebounds, and 0.8 assists per game. At the conclusion of the season, he was selected to the NBA All-Rookie Second Team.

On October 17, 2017, Brown tallied a then-career-high 25 points in the Celtics’ 102-99 loss against the Cavaliers in the team’s season opener for the 2017–18 campaign. He failed to get off to a good start in the 2018–19 season, missing the first three games with a bruised lower back. On the sixth of December, he returned and scored 21 points in a 128–100 victory against the New York Knicks.

At age 21, he became the Celtics’ youngest player in history to score 30 or more points in a playoff game. In a 120-111 loss to the San Antonio Spurs on December 31st, he scored a season-high 30 points and agreed to a four-year, $115 million contract deal with the Boston Celtics.

The Celtics reached the Eastern Conference Finals for the third time during Brown’s four seasons in the NBA after winning a series against the Philadelphia 76ers and Toronto Raptors in four and seven games, respectively. He narrowly missed being selected for the 2020 NBA All-Star Game in January.

However, the Miami Heat eliminated him in the Conference Finals after six games. Jaylen was chosen as a reserve for the 2021 All-Star team after recording a then-career-high 42 points, five rebounds, and four assists in a 126-107 victory over the Memphis Grizzlies on December 30, 2020. On February 24, 2021, he was officially named an NBA All-Star for the first time.

With only four regular-season games remaining, his career-best campaign came to an end when he needed wrist surgery to repair a torn scapholunate ligament in his left wrist. Brown set a Celtics franchise record for points on an opening night on October 30, 2021, when scoring a then-career-high 46 points in a 138-134 double-overtime loss to the New York Knicks.

On January 2, 2022, he surpassed that mark by tallying 50 points and 11 rebounds in a 116-111 overtime victory over the Orlando Magic. On March 18, 2022, in a 126-97 victory over the Sacramento Kings, he and Jayson Tatum each scored at least 30 points for the eighth time in their careers, tying Larry Bird and Kevin McHale of the Boston Celtics for the most such games with four in the 1986–1987 NBDL.

In the following game, when the Denver Nuggets were defeated 124-104, Jaylen and Jayson Tatum eclipsed the previous mark by both scoring 30 points on almost 60% of their field goal attempts. As a senior in the Class 6A State Championship of the Georgia High School Association (GHSA), Jaylen Brown begins his career by guiding his team to victory.

He helped Wheeler to a 30-3 overall record while averaging 28 points and 12 rebounds per game. As a member of the USA Basketball Men’s U18 National Team, he won a gold medal at the 2014 FIBA Americas Championship. He was also chosen to participate in the 2015 McDonald’s All-American Boys Game.

He received a five-star rating and was listed as the fourth-best prospect in his class by Scout, ESPN, and 247Sports. On May 1, 2015, he also made the decision to play for the University of California, Berkeley Golden Bears. Because of his athleticism, he was regarded as an all-around talent at the time.

In 34 games for the Golden Bears in 2015–2016, Jaylen averaged 14.6 points, 5.4 rebounds, and 2.0 assists in 27.6 minutes per contest. He scored 27 points against Utah on January 27, 2016, and 27 points against Richmond on November 27, 2015, respectively.
In a 74-73 victory over Arizona on January 23, 2016, he produced a season-high 7 assists in addition to 15 points, earning first-team All-Pac-12 accolades and the Pac-12 Freshman of the Year award.
