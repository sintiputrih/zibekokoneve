Matt B. Britton Biography

Matt B. Britton is one of the most popular and richest Businessperson who was born on January 24, 1975 in Lafayette Hill, Pennsylvania, United States. In July of 2011, Britton executed the spinoff of CrowdTap which was the social media marketing platform. In the following months, CrowdTap has raised $7 million in Series A Financing through Foundry Group. Foundry Group. The company also secured another $5 million in Series B financing during 2014, from Foundry Group and others. Britton was with CrowdTap in the role of Chief Advisor, from July until February 2017. In February of 2017 Britton was appointed CEO of the company.

In April 2015 Britton published his first book entitled “YouthNation” with Wiley Publishing. The book explores the impact millennials have on the future of business and brand building. The book peaked at #8 on the Los Angeles Times bestsellers list.

In March 2018, Britton executed the spinoff of Suzy, a real-time consumer intelligence software platform. Suzy completed a $5 Million Series B funding in late 2017 from the Durant Company and Foundry Group. Suzy’s clients include Netflix, Coca-Cola, Procter & Gamble and Nintendo. Britton was named to the Digital 40 under 40 (the top 40 U.S. digital executives under 40 years of age.)

The month of September, 2002 saw Britton was a partner with Paul Tedeschi to establish Mr Youth LLC, a youth-focused marketing agency. In 2005, the company of Britton Mr. Youth bought the 23-year-old CollegeFest event. In 2005 and 2006, Britton became a household name by appearing as an expert on the show hosted by Jon Stewart regarding youth marketing. The month of February, 2009 saw Mr Youth closed private equity financing in partnership with Alta Communications and The Mustang Group when Paul Tedeschi left the company In 2009, Britton was also named as a “modern day Don Draper” by the NY Post. In March 2010, Britton’s agency Mr Youth was named one of the top 10 most innovative Advertising/Marketing companies in the world In March 2011, Britton announced the S-List a partnership with former NBA Star Rick Fox to leverage the use of social media by celebrities into branding opportunities. In November of 2011, Britton sold Mr Youth to LBi for an estimated between $40 and $50 million.

Britton was selected to”the Entrepreneur Magazine “30 Under 30” list (the most successful thirty U.S. entrepreneurs under 30 years old) in April 2000.

Britton currently serves as Chief Executive Officer of Suzy, a real-time consumer intelligence software. Previously he served as Chief Executive Officer of MRY, a marketing and technology agency with over 500 employees that is part of the Publicis Groupe. MRY, formed in 2013, is the combination of Mr. Youth, (the agency Britton founded in 2002) and the former U.S. operations of LBi. The two agencies merged under Britton’s leadership in January 2013 to become MRY. MRY’s clients include Visa, Coca-Cola, Bayer, and Neutrogena.
