Ann Curry’s Background

Ann Curry, born on 19 November 1956 in Guam, is a television personality and journalist of mixed descent – Cherokee,  Scottish, French, German, Irish (father) and Japanese (mother). She gained recognition as a television anchor for the NBC network.

Ann Curry’s estimated net worth is over $10 million as of mid-2016, derived mainly from her involvement in the entertainment industry, particularly from her role in the morning NBC television show, “The Today Show”, where she reportedly earned an annual salary of $2 million.

Professional Journey

Ann Curry began her career at the KTVL station in an internship role, eventually rising to become the first female news reporter there. Subsequently, she worked at KGW and KCBS-TV where she received two Emmy Awards. Curry gained fame in 1990 when she joined the “NBC News” broadcast network, later becoming a correspondent and anchor of “NBC News at Sunrise”. She also appeared as a co-anchor on “Dateline NBC” and as anchor on “NBC Nightly News”.

Throughout her career, Curry reported from various countries and interviewed influential figures such as former U.S. Army General Tommy Franks and former Iranian President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad.

In 2012, Curry left “The Today Show” after nearly fourteen years and currently works as an NBC News National and International Correspondent, as well as NBC’s News Special Anchor.

Personal Life

Ann Curry has been married to Brian Ross for over 30 years, and they have two children. She is also actively involved in charities such as “Save the Children”, “Susan G. Komen Breast Cancer Foundation”, and the “Multiple Myeloma Research Foundation”.
