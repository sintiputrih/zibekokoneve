Early life

The 61-year-old Herschel was born on March 3, 1962. He was born in Augusta, Georgia. He was raised in Wrightsville, Georgia. Similarly, he holds an American nationality.

Likewise, His parents are his father Willis Walker, and his mother Christine Walker. He was born in a family of nine including six of his siblings.

The names of his siblings are; Willis Walker Jr., Veronica Richard, Lorenza Walker, Carol Walker, Evangelist Sharon King, and Renneth Walker.

In his childhood, He suffered from obesity and stuttered while speaking. However, his mother motivated him to do better in life and not use those as excuses if he ever fails in life.

Awards and Nominations

Herschel Walker was indeed an extraordinary player. He won numerous awards throughout his entire career as a footballer.

The first-ever award he won was the 1982 Heisman Award, which is awarded to the most outstanding college football player of a particular year. Similarly, the same year he was also awarded the Maxwell Award.

Later in the same year, he also won the Walter Camp Player of the Year.

Career

College Career

His career started in high school, where he found his never before known interest in sports. He is to date one of the most impressive players in the history of college games.

He led his team in both the sports of football and track and field during his high school years into state championships. While being so good at sports, he never lost his focus on his studies.

He was always a good student and did well in his academics as well as performed brilliantly in sports.

After joining the University of Georgia for his further studies, he set a record by being the only player in the history of the NCAA to finish in the top three in Heisman voting in three of his collegiate seasons.

Similarly, he was the first-ever freshman to become a first-team-all-American. He led his team from Georgia University to many victories against other teams in various championships.

However, he put an end to his college career as a college football player after he turned professional.

Professional career

He signed with the New Jersey Generals in the year 1983. Also, he performed phenomenally in the league, to the point where he dominated the entire league.

He set the records in professional football for a single season. Later in his career, he joined the Dallas Cowboys in the year 1986.  At one of the highest points during his career, the Cowboys traded Him to the Minnesota Vikings.

However, 4 years later, he joined the Philadelphia Eagles in the year 1992. he soon left Philadelphia after player for three seasons. He was signed by the New York Giants for a $4.8 million contract.

He brought an end to his career as a professional football player in the year 1997 after ending the season with the Dallas Cowboys.
