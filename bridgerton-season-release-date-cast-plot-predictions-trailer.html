Bridgerton Season 4 Release Date: When will it Air?

Bridgerton has been renewed for a third and fourth season on Netflix, opening the door for a third and fourth season of the period drama. Season 2 overtook Season 1 as Netflix’s most popular English-language show, featuring Jonathan Bailey and Simon Ashley.

The period drama Bridgerton Season 1 premiered on the Netflix platform on Christmas Day, 25 December 2020. It is an eight-episode series of unadulterated entertainment that runs for hours.

A release date has not yet been set. Netflix won’t give a release date because the season hasn’t yet been filmed.

Colin and Penelope said goodbye in the first season when Colin left to travel, as Penelope had always urged him to do. Penelope was about to make her own “I burn for you”-style proclamation of love, though. According to the book’s summary, the plot picks up when Colin returns to London and they learn truths about one another that put their chances of a happy ending to the test. Penelope’s secret is well known, but it will be intriguing to learn what Colin is hiding.

Despite not having much of a presence in the books, Season 1’s Maria Thompson was a significant candidate for Colin’s affections. The second season, however, demonstrated that there is no longer any tension between them. Penelope appeared to be prepared to reveal her genuine love for the longstanding family friend now that Marina was out of the picture, but at the end of Season 2, she overheard him say that there was no chance he would ever go for her.

Bridgerton Season 2 Review:

Both critics and viewers have had varied reactions to Bridgerton season 2. Despite the bright-yellow gowns, the luxurious venue, and the melodramatic hairstyles, some critics have complimented the performance for its searing passion and delicious intrigue while others have criticised it for being lacklustre, modest, and somber.

Bridgerton Season 2 Rating:

Bridgerton season 2 has a user score of 58 based on 58 ratings and a Metacritic score of 70 based on 32 reviews from critics. According to Netflix’s recently revealed Top 10 rankings for the week of March 21–27, the show received 193 million hours of viewing during its first weekend.
