Career and Professional Life

Sibylle started her profession in the early 1980s after migrating from Germany to London. She took out a contract on a working warehouse in London’s Lower East Side, which she quickly converted into her first painting studio.

Other artists who contributed to Sibylle’s work were in the same room as her. Later, she began organizing group shows in Germany and England. At Hamburg’s “Galerie in Theatre Operrettenhaus,” Sibylle produced her first group show in 1989.

Additionally, in the late 1980s, Sibylle moved to the US. She gained a lot of knowledge about Native American culture and its spiritual connection there. Her subsequent series of paintings included the lessons she had learned.

Furthermore, early in the 1990s, Sibylle moved to Utah and opened a painting studio in the mountain community of Sundance. She set up her first solo show in Salt Lake City in 1991.

Sibylle visited several historic cities and villages in Morocco in 2000. The peaceful scenery of Draa Valley moved her.

She drew on her expertise to create a collection of oil sketches and images that prominently featured Morocco’s history and heritage. The artwork was displayed in San Francisco not long after the sad 9/11 event.

More about career

Sibylle founded The Way of the Rain, Inc. as her non-profit company in November 2015. The organization works to assist the growth of up-and-coming artists and raises awareness of the need to safeguard the environment.

Moreover, for the “US Embassy” in Paramaribo, Suriname, Sibylle received a 2016 Art in Embassies Award from the US State Department. Likewise, ten of her masterpieces were chosen to be on permanent exhibit in the embassy.

In Santa Fe, New Mexico, the “Ernesto Mayans Gallery” and the “Niman Fine Art” are now featuring Sibylle’s artwork.
