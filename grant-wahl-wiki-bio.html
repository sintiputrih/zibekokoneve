Grant Wahl Education, School, University

Grant Wahl was a Soccer fan since his childhood. He finished his schooling at Shawnee Mission East High School and graduated. While in school he was a member of the Eagle Scouts. Grant studied Politics at Princeton University and earned a Bachelor’s Degree in Arts in Politics.

Grant Wahl Career, Sports

Wahl used to cover sports events while in college and that’s how he was interested in Sports. He initially worked as an intern with the Miami Herald before he joined  Sports Illustrated in November 1996, covering college basketball as well as soccer.

As a part of Sports Illustrated, he covered NCAA Basketball Tournaments, 8 FIFA men’s World Cups,  4 FIFA Women’s World Cups, and also 5 Olympic games. He started the famous cover story called “Where’s Daddy?” Which documented the growing number of illegitimate children of Sports personalities. Grant was later promoted to senior writer at Sports Illustrated and covered even more events and wrote many more cover stories.

In 2011, he ran for the President of FIFA, however, he didn’t win. Grant joined FOX Sports in 2012 after he participated in the network coverage of the UEFA Euro 2012 tournament. However, in 2020, he was fired from Sports Illustrated after he criticized James Heckman for his handling of pay cuts during the Pandemic of 2020. After that, he worked as an analyst for CBS Sports and covered CONCACAF soccer matches. He also had his own podcast series. He was also covering the 2022 FIFA World Cup.

Grant Wahl FIFA 2022

He was covering the 2022 FIFA World Cup when he was caught up in a crackdown in Qatar on items featuring the LGBT community symbol, the Rainbow. Moreover, Grant along with the New York Times Reporter who was covering him was detained outside a stadium for 25 minutes. He published the last entry entitled “They Just Don’t Care” criticizing the Qatar officials for their apathy towards the laborers at the different construction sites in the country.

Grant Wahl Books, Death

Grant wrote several cover stories which were widely read, criticized, and appreciated and he was a very good writer. He also published his own book called “The Beckham Experiment” in 2008. Post to the success of his first book, he published his second one entitled “Master of Modern Soccer: How the World’s Best Play the Twenty-First-Century Game” in 2018.

While covering the quarterfinal match between Argentina and Netherlands in FIFA World Cup 2022, he suddenly collapsed in his press box seat at the Qatar Stadium. Even before he could be taken to the hospital he was pronounced dead. His brother Eric, however, claimed that Grant was killed and stated that he and his Gmail are in contact with the U.S. State Department and White House Officials. Grant’s body went through Autopsy and the cause of death was due to Aortic Aneurysm. Earlier, when he mentioned his chest discomfort to the medical clinic at the World Cup, he was said that he might have Bronchitis and was given cough syrup and Antibiotics. Later, FIFA president, Gianni Infantino issued a statement in his honor followed by other Sports personalities who shared their condolences.
