Meet Scarlet May, The Deaf TikToker Mocked By PewDiePie

On 26 July 2022, YouTuber PewDiePie shared a video titled “My Dog Cringes at TikToks,” in which he reacts to viral videos with his pup, Maya. Creator Scarlet May, who is deaf and frequently uploads TikToks using both spoken English and sign language, was included in one of those clips. She frequently has long nails when communicating with her fans.

She posted a video, which PewDiePie noticed and cut short. “I’m not listening to this,” the YouTuber said in his video. “Look, she has your crazy nails, Maya!” Then he appeared to replicate American Sign Language by waving both of the dog’s paws around. “Sorry, are my nails distracting you guys,” PewDiePie continued.

The video received criticism from viewers, one of whom tweeted, “Pewdiepie isn’t ableist, he just wanted to mock a Black woman for millions of viewers for having long fingernails and speaking in her natural dialect! It’s ONLY racism”.

After learning that Scarlet is actually a deaf TikTok creator, he issued an apology.

“Hey, just to clear a few things up: I edited out the clip with the girl that has the long nails,” he commented on his YouTube video on July 27. “Had no clue she was deaf but kinda dumb of me to not realize. Still watching through the clip I only poked fun at her long nails. The voice I did for my dog is the same voice I’ve given her for years.”

PewDiePie also said that making his dog’s paws move “is an ongoing theme in the video,” adding, “Anyway honest mistake, my bad”.

May also addressed it in her recent video on TikTok.

Did You Know: Scarlet May lost her hearing ability at the age of 3. She then learned ASL to communicate.

Deaf TikToker Scarlet May Job

TikToker Scarlet May is a social media influencer and advocate of the deaf community. She is one of the most successful influencers with 6.2 million followers on her TikTok. She blends entertainment, ASL musical covers, and Deaf education seamlessly. Rev.com listed her as one of the “10 Deaf TikTok You Can’t Miss” in September 2021.

Her “Deaf Kardashians” series, in which she recreates famous Kardashian moments with outfit changes and the ideal “crying Kim” expression, is where she first gained popularity. Her success with Deaf Kardashians also got her to be interviewed by Nylon Magazine.

Speaking about how she got the concept, she shared, “When I first started TikTok, I was in college in D.C. My friends were telling me about a girl named Charli [D’Amelio]. At that point, I had never heard of TikTok or anything like that, so I decided to download the app. I was looking over it and thought it was really cool I first started with dancing, I figured it would be enjoyable, like why not? I made one of the “Deaf Kardashians” series and that went viral. It was my first video to go viral, and then from there on I’ve been doing that ever since”.

Moreover, Scarlet also has 183K followers on Instagram. The sizable followers allowed her to become a fashion model landing paid promotions with several apparel brands.

Scarlet graduated from Model Secondary School for the Deaf in Washington D.C. During her high school years, she was in Jr.NAD, ASBG, and Class Officer. She also played volleyball, softball, track, cheerleading, and basketball. She received Gallaudet University Dean’s Prestige $6,000/year scholarship to attend Gallaudet University.
